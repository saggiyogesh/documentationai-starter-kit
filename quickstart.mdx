## Prerequisites

<Callout kind="info" title="Before You Begin">
You need a modern web browser like Chrome, Firefox, or Safari. No additional software installation is required. Ensure you have a stable internet connection for account registration.
</Callout>

## Step-by-Step Setup

Follow these steps to register, log in, create your first documentation space, add a project, and make your initial edits.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Register Your Account" icon="user-plus">
    Visit `https://app.yogeshagrawal.com/register` and provide your email address and a secure password.

    Create a strong password with at least 12 characters, including uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols.

    Click **Sign Up** to receive a verification email. Check your inbox (and spam folder) and click the verification link.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Log In to Your Dashboard" icon="log-in">
    Navigate to `https://app.yogeshagrawal.com/login`.

    Enter your email and password, then click **Log In**.

    You'll land on the dashboard where you can manage your documentation spaces.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a New Documentation Space" icon="plus">
    From the dashboard, click **New Space**.

    Enter a name like `My Project Docs` and select a description: "Organize all project documentation here."

    Choose your brand color `#3B82F6` and click **Create Space**.

    Your new space appears in the list, ready for projects.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add Your First Project" icon="folder-plus">
    Inside your space, click **Add Project**.

    Name it `Project Alpha` and add a brief description: "Core project documentation."

    Optionally, upload a logo or set permissions for team members.

    Click **Save** to generate the project structure with default pages like `introduction.mdx`.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Edit and Save Your First Page" icon="edit-3">
    Open the `introduction.mdx` file in the built-in editor.

    Add content using Markdown or MDX syntax (see example below).

    Preview changes in real-time, then click **Save** or use `Ctrl+S` (`Cmd+S` on macOS).

    Your changes publish automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Basic Editing Tips

Use the intuitive editor for Markdown and MDX. Here's a quick example of a frontmatter block and content.

<CodeGroup tabs="MDX Example">
  ```mdx
---
title: My Page
description: Page description here.
---

## Overview

Welcome to your documentation!
  ```
</CodeGroup>

  <Tab title="Keyboard Shortcuts" icon="keyboard">

    - `Ctrl+/` (`Cmd+/`): Toggle comments
    - `Ctrl+Shift+P` (`Cmd+Shift+P`): Open command palette for components
